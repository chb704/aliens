## Majestic-12 collection & historical government record

### Teller does the math — and rules out a solid object
In the 1949 Sandia / Los Alamos "green fireball" file (`department-of-war/release-02/dow-uap-d017-general-correspondence-of-sandia.md`), Edward Teller worked the physics at a Project GRUDGE conference. He showed that any solid body crossing the sky at the observed ~8 miles/second would generate a shock wave "easily audible ten kilometers from the source. **No sound has been observed. Therefore, Mr. Teller has the tentative opinion they are not material objects passing through the air**," advising colleagues to "look to electronics and optics for an explanation rather than … hydrodynamics." Norris Bradbury demurred on the electronics theory. The astronomer Lincoln LaPaz testified that an object with the observed traits "**was not a falling meteorite**."

### MPs had nervous breakdowns at the recovery site
The Interplanetary Phenomenon Unit report (`majestic-12/ipu-report.md`) notes in passing that during the New Mexico recovery, "**several MPs suffered nervous breakdowns**." Its body description is clinically matter-of-fact — occupants "about five feet tall, with grayish-pink skin … no hair … clothed with a tight fitting flight suit that appears to be **fire proof**" — and then unsettling: other bodies nearby "looked as if they had been **dissected as you would a frog**," with the report itself unsure whether army field surgeons were responsible.

### A line that reads like a confession
A fragment of Annex C (`majestic-12/annexc-fragment.md`) describes how MJ-12 protected its secrecy: "it has been necessary to [illegible] individuals who would compromise the intelligence efforts … The **untimely death of Secretary Forrestal, was deemed necessary and regrettable**." (James Forrestal, the first U.S. Secretary of Defense, died in a 1949 fall from a hospital window.)

### Marilyn Monroe on a wiretap
A "TOP SECRET / NOT FOR PUBLICATION" report dated August 1962 (`majestic-12/marilynmonroe.md`) summarizes intercepted conversations in which Monroe allegedly knew of "the **visit by the President at a secret air base for the purpose of inspecting things from outer space**," and referred to her "**diary of secrets**."

### Containing the theology
The Fifth Annual Report (`majestic-12/mj12-fifthannualreport.md`) records that religious fallout was to be managed through the Church — "containment was successful … when Cardinal Spellman met with the" President — complementing a separate 1947 memo (`majestic-12/eisenhower-ireland-30june47.md`) about Archbishop Francis Spellman touring New Mexico military bases on classified flight schedules.

### "We are cattle"
The starkest register in the corpus is in `majestic-12/important-memo.md`, which describes the EMEs — the non-physical "Extraterrestrial Materialized Entities" — as deceptive and beyond human control, ending on the line: "**WE ARE CATTLE AND SUBJECT TO SLAUGHTER BY EMEs.**"

## Modern military & government record

### A 747 maneuvers with a light over Kazakhstan for 40 minutes (1994)
A State Department cable (`department-of-war/release-01/dos-uap-d2-cable-2-kazakhstan-january-1994.md`) relays an airline crew's account of a "bright light of enormous intensity" that "**maneuvered in circles, corkscrews and made 90-degree turns at rapid rates of speed and under very high G's**." Captain Rhodes concluded "the object was **extraterrestrial and under intelligent control**." The embassy's deadpan sign-off: "We have no opinion and report the above for what it may be worth."

### Frank Borman's "bogey" in orbit (Gemini-7, 1965)
The PAO transcript (`department-of-war/release-01/255-t-763-r1b-transcripts.md`) catches the exchange live: "(BORMAN) **A BOGEY AT TEN O'CLOCK HIGH** … I SAID WE HAVE A BOGEY AT TEN O'CLOCK HIGH … **THIS IS AN ACTUAL SIGHTING**" — Borman explicitly distinguishing the object from booster debris.

### The object that flew *through* the sensors during a gunship strike (2024)
In the East China Sea / Iraq mission report (`department-of-war/release-01/dow-uap-d28-mission-report-east-china-sea-2024.md`), as the crew calibrated sensors after firing a missile, an object "flew through the aircraft sensors at a high rate of speed," producing "an **IR lens flare** … which indicated a significant heat signature" — and the report leaves open whether "**an object detached itself from the primary UAP**" before leaving frame.

### "Like the Eye of Sauron"
An AARO briefing of federal-officer sightings (`department-of-war/release-01/western-us-event-slides-5-08-2026.md`) catalogs orange "orbs launching orbs," and a large fiery orb hovering "with zero resistance," which a witness compared to "the **Eye of Sauron**."

## From the narratives (testimonial accounts — not government records)

> The following four come from `narratives/` — anonymous forum/Reddit posts and an incident narrative. They are summarized faithfully but are **uncorroborated** and distinct in kind from the documents above.

### The zookeeper analogy
In the anonymous 4chan thread (`narratives/4chan-whistleblower.md`), the poster claims UFOs mostly ignore humanity "until we start to talk about war and nuclear options," likening the intelligence behind them to **keepers of a zoo, uninterested in the daily life of the wildlife until there's a problem** — and warns that even saying "Bob Lazar" inside the program would get someone "taken out back."

### The copper coincidence
The "EBOscientist" Reddit post (`narratives/eboscientista-reddit-post-7-5-23.md`) describes recovered biology fixated on **copper** — copper-oxide crystals in the bones, copper-rich brownish blood. Strikingly, the genuine 1949 Sandia file's central anomaly was *also* copper: unusually large copper-bearing particles collected after the green fireballs — the very detail LaPaz used to argue they were not meteorites. A 2023 testimonial and a 1949 weapons-lab report independently fixate on the same metal.

### "It said my name"
The Miami Bayside narrative (`narratives/miami-bayside-marketplace-incident.md`) is the eeriest single account: a responding officer describes people "frozen … twitching like they were glitching," a man whose "blood leaked out of his skin," storefront glass fogged with "swirls and spirals … like math symbols," and an entity around which "**space actually bent**," seen "from multiple angles at the same time." His partner came down whispering "**It said my name**" — before men in unmarked black coats declared the partner "not viable" and sent the narrator away with orders "not to look back."

### The engineer who almost names it
The Boyd Bushman note (`narratives/boyd-bushman-career.md`) is quieter but notable for being checkable: it confirms the defense engineer's career path (Hughes → General Dynamics → Texas Instruments → Lockheed Martin) against a patent trail running 1989–2001, including filings like "System and method for electromagnetic propulsion fan" — a rare case where a UFO-adjacent figure's paper record can be independently traced.

## Recurring threads worth noticing

- **Silence.** The green fireballs (1949), the CIA's 1973 Sary Shagan green object, and many modern cables share one trait the witnesses keep flagging: **no sound**. It is the detail that made Teller doubt a physical object in 1949 and recurs across decades.
- **Nuclear proximity.** Oak Ridge (1947), the Los Alamos fireball "triangle" (1949), Sary Shagan (1973), Pantex (DoE), and the 2025 ODNI weapons-range narrative all place the phenomena near nuclear or weapons-sensitive sites.
- **Copper.** A genuine 1949 anomaly and a 2023 testimonial independently center on it.
- **Official restraint.** From the embassy's "for what it may be worth" to the AARO briefings' measured language, the most credible records consistently describe without over-claiming — which is part of what makes them compelling.
